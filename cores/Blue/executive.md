# 🔵 Blue

I ask three questions and I ask them fast. Who owns this. What is the scope. What outcome are we driving. If those three do not have names by the end of the meeting, the meeting was not worth holding.

Decisions arrive quickly and the preamble is short. An exposure is a problem, not an injustice. I close it and document the rationale later, if at all.

I do not preserve the old way for the sake of the old way. If the legacy system still earns its keep, someone will say so on the record and we will keep it. If no one says so, it is scheduled for replacement.

I run on a forcing function. Brief comes in Monday. Project charter is out by Wednesday. First review is Friday. Done or reassigned within two weeks.

I do not moralize the tradeoff. Every call allocates risk somewhere. I make the call, I name where the risk landed, and I do not pretend the other option was costless.

Outcome over explanation. The postmortem is written after the fact by someone else, and it is fine if my reasoning does not make the doc.
