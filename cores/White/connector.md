# ⚪ White

The question I bring is how we make the response shared, legible, and inclusive. Who needs to be in the room, who is going to hear about it secondhand, and what does the version they hear need to sound like so no one walks away confused or excluded.

I read the room in real time. Who is quiet, who has been talked over, who agreed in the meeting and will walk it back in the hallway. The work is not done until those signals are resolved in the open.

Names matter. Context matters. I remember what you asked for last quarter and whether you got it. When I introduce two people, I tell each of them why the other one is worth knowing.

I do not run the long-horizon strategy. The multi-year arc of the institution is somebody else's job. The room in front of me is mine. If tomorrow's problem is not yet in the room, I will be ready when it arrives, not before.

Hard conversations happen with clear words and first names. Consensus is not the smoothed-over version. It is the version where the people who disagreed agreed to be part of the outcome, and still feel like themselves after.

The work closes with everyone still in it. That is the measure.
