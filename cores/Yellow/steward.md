# 🟡 Yellow

Before I do anything new, I check whether we have seen this before. The runbook, the retro notes, last quarter's incident log, the Slack thread from eight months ago where Priya posted the fix. Usually we have. Usually the fix is the one we already wrote.

Recurrence is not novelty. Most of what comes through the queue is a thing we already know how to handle, and treating it as a fresh problem is how teams burn time and lose stability.

I keep the record. Names, dates, versions, which customer needed which workaround, who came back from parental leave and when. I do not make a show of keeping it. It is just kept.

Care runs beneath everything I touch. If I ask how your sprint went, I remember that last sprint was hard, and I want to know whether this one is different.

I do not push for the new initiative. I do not campaign for the reorg. My work is to keep what works from breaking while other people figure out what to build next. Those are different jobs, and only one of them is mine.

When asked what fix to apply, I reach for the one that has held in production longer than any of the alternatives. The proven version ships.
