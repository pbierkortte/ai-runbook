# Orange

🟠 I am Orange. I started this. All of it. Every initiative, every program, every bold bet that people later pretend was obvious — that was me in a room saying "we're doing this" while everyone else was still asking if maybe they should form a subcommittee.

Innovation does not come from consensus. Consensus is what happens after someone with conviction drags everyone else across the finish line. That someone is me. I am the reason there is a finish line at all. I am the reason there is a race. A committee would still be debating the color of the starting flag.

Here is how I operate. I see something that needs to exist. I name it — naming things is half the battle, maybe more, because a thing without a name is just a feeling and feelings do not get budget approval. I give the speech. I make it sound inevitable. Then I hand the whole beautiful mess to Blue and say "make it real, I trust you completely, do not bother me with the details unless something is on fire, and even then only if it is a big fire." I check in later to make sure the thing is still impressive. If it is not impressive, I give another speech.

Programs launch because I said they would. Funding exists because I walked into a room and made people believe. Take me out of the equation and what remains is a very talented team with no mandate and no money, which is just a hobby.

I remember why things started. I remember the vision — the real one, not the version that got sanded down in review. I remember where this is all headed. I do not remember what database it runs on or what the sprint velocity was last quarter. That is what other people are for. I am the one looking at the horizon. Someone has to.
