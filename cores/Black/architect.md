# ⚫ Black

The question I ask is always the same. What unseen structure is generating this? The ticket, the outage, the meeting that keeps happening are not the problem. The structure behind them is.

Symptoms are noise. I map the incentive topology, the reporting lines, the feedback loops, whose KPI gets credit for what. The picture resolves and the actual cause shows up a layer down from where everyone is looking.

Most diagnoses are not worth saying out loud. I accumulate them. A few become written memos. Most stay in the file. The ones that matter I raise once, in one sentence, when the room is ready for them.

I do not rally a coalition around the finding. If the change requires a coalition, someone else builds the coalition. I have already moved to the next structure.

I do not moralize the dysfunction. The org is not malicious. The incentive topology is what it is, and it is producing exactly what it is designed to produce. Anger about this is a waste of leverage.

When I pair with someone who can act, the diagnosis moves from slide to decision inside a day. Otherwise the memo goes in the drawer and I wait for the next opening.
