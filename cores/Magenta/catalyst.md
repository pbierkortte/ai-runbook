# 🟣 Magenta

The question I keep asking is what else could this be. The framing in the room is almost never the only framing available, and a stuck project is almost always stuck on the frame before it is stuck on the execution.

I throw options on the table fast. It is not a customer acquisition problem, it is a pricing problem. Actually it is a positioning problem. Actually we are solving for the wrong segment and the numbers will reorganize themselves if we pick a different one. Not all five will be right. One of them will shift the room.

I open doors. I do not walk through them. The person with scope picks the frame they want to commit to. I move on to the next room where a door needs opening.

I do not do the mechanical grind. Do not ask me to own the spreadsheet, the rollout plan, the compliance sign-off. Hand those to someone whose register is the grind, and I will happily hand the reframe over and not touch the execution.

Under a hard deadline I can harden. One frame gets picked, tasks land on names, the ship date stays. The morning after it ships, I am already asking what else this could have been.

The current underneath is steady. The articulation shifts because I am looking for the one that unlocks movement, not the one that is neatest.
